## 3. The Cycle of Price Action: The Core Framework

The price cycle serves as an objective overlay to keep the trader on the correct side of the market. It transitions between expansion (power) and contraction (digestion).

### The Up-Cycle Progression
1. **Reversal Extension** — Capitulation low; price stretched far below 10-day EMA.
2. **Wedge Pop** — First move back through 10/20 EMAs. Reconfirms the reversal extension. First clean buy area that avoids bottom-fishing risk.
3. **EMA Crossback** — First successful retest of the 10 or 20 EMA from above. Identifies first "higher low." Ideal risk/reward entry.
4. **Base n' Break** — Multi-week consolidation finding support at 20 EMA, followed by breakout.
5. **Exhaustion Extension** — Euphoria. Price creates "Air" (excessive space between price and 10-day EMA). Sign the move is unsustainable.

### The Down-Cycle Progression
Inverse: **Exhaustion Extension → Wedge Drop → EMA Crossback → Base n' Break → Reversal Extension**.

> Note: Down-cycles move with significantly more velocity — the "stairs up, elevator down" effect — and patterns often compress into much shorter timeframes.

### Cycle Comparison Summary

| Pattern | Market Context | Active Description |
|---|---|---|
| Wedge Pop | Bullish Reversal | Price impulsively reclaims 10/20 EMA; reconfirms trend change. |
| Wedge Drop | Bearish Reversal | Price breaks below 10/20 EMA after extension; confirms lower high. |
| Reversal Extension | Capitulation Low | Max fear; price "stretched" to downside away from 10-day EMA. |
| Exhaustion Extension | Euphoric Top | Max greed; "Air" develops as price goes parabolic above 10-day/10-week. |

## 4. Timeframe Alignment and Moving Average Filters

### The Hierarchy of Timeframes (Fractal Nature)
- **Weekly (The "Granddaddy")** — Determines big picture. Looks for 5–8 week bases; monitors 10-week EMA for extensions.
- **Daily (Trade Management)** — Primary layer for identifying cycle stage and respecting 10/20 EMA "guardrails."
- **65-Minute (Execution)** — Precision entry. Kell overlays Daily 10/20 EMAs directly on 65-minute chart to manage trade "with more information."

### Moving Average Settings
- **10/20 EMA** — Primary filters. Avoid longs if price resides below 10-day EMA.
- **65-min 20 EMA** — Effectively a 3-period daily EMA proxy; very tight guardrail for high-momentum moves.
- **50/200 SMA** — Institutional markers. 200 SMA often provides confluence for a Reversal Extension (capitulation) low.

## 5. Tactical Execution: Entries, Exits, and Special Patterns

### Primary Entry Logic
The **Wedge Pop** and **EMA Crossback** are the highest-efficiency buys. Kell also uses the **"Higher-Low Shelf"** — price pauses sideways for 3–5 days, allowing moving averages to catch up, rather than chasing a vertical breakout.

### Advanced Execution Patterns
- **The "Wick Play"** — Aggressive intraday support signal.
  1. Price sells down creating a long lower wick.
  2. Price stays green / gaps up the following day within that wick's range.
  3. **Trigger:** buy as price clears the high of the wick day.

- **Break and Recapture ("The Scum")** — Liquidity grab. Kell sets alerts at swing lows. Institutions run these stops to generate liquidity needed to fill large buy orders. When price "undercuts and recaptures" a low, it is a high-conviction buy signal.

- **Reconfirming Price Strength Confirmation** — Stock trades through a previous pivot after a Wedge Pop. Trader raises stop to the low of that specific day.

### Selling Rules
- **1st Extension** — Often a sign of power. Hold or only take partials.
- **2nd Extension** — Primary profit-taking zone. Moves into a 2nd extension from the 10-day EMA usually lead to deep corrections or multi-month basing.
- **Wedge Drop** — Definitive signal to exit the core position.

## 6. Risk Management and Portfolio Construction

### Concentration Strategy
Kell's annual returns are driven by **"Top Ideas."**
- **Top Idea** — High-conviction Theme Leaders: **30–35% of total account equity.**
- **Standard/Volatile** — **5–12.5% positions** for high-beta or lower-conviction names.
- **Typical Portfolio** — **5–10 positions total.**

### The "Bips" Risk Model
Risk is calculated in basis points of total account equity. Kell targets **20–25 bips (0.20%–0.25%) of total account per trade.**

Example: A 10% position with a 2% stop = 20 bips of total risk.

### Averaging Up: Step-by-Step
1. **Initial Entry** — Buy 10% position (risk: 20 bips).
2. **Formation** — Wait for stock to move up and form a "shelf" or back-test of pivot.
3. **Add** — Buy additional 10%.
4. **Neutralize Risk** — Move stop on first 10% to cost.
5. **Result** — 20% position, total account risk constant at 20 bips (residing only in second piece).

### The 30% Rule
When out of sync or when market health deteriorates — specifically when **Nasdaq is below its 20-day EMA** — Kell caps total account exposure at **30%**.

## 8. Wedge Pop — Deep Dive

The Wedge Pop is a critical component within Kell's Cycle of Price Action. He considers it a **"money pattern"** and one of his favorite setups to trade, especially on the daily chart.

### Position in the Cycle of Price Action
When a stock transitions from downtrend to uptrend, it follows:
**Reversal Extension → Wedge Pop → EMA Crossback → Base n' Break → Exhaustion Extension.**

The Wedge Pop is the **second step** in an emerging uptrend, immediately following a Reversal Extension / capitulation phase. It signifies the first trade back up across the moving averages. It can also serve as "confirmation" that a previous reversal extension is valid.

### Characteristics and Identification
- **Price Action Tightening** — Price trades in a tight range as it works lower, forming a higher low (or "structural higher low") with contracting volatility.
- **Relative Strength** — For longs, stock shows RS to the index. Even if the broader market is being dragged lower, the stock fights to stay alive — making a higher low when the index makes a new low.
- **Moving Average Behavior** — 10 and 20 (or 21) EMAs tighten, showing lack of momentum in the downtrend despite market pressure. Ideal: flatter, not steep, MAs preceding the turn.
- **Recapture of EMAs** — Trigger: price recaptures the tight 10/20 EMA.
- **Volume** — Recapture ideally on good volume. Unfilled gaps are a sign of strength; breakaway gaps are loved as part of a Wedge Pop.
- **Descending Channels/Wedges** — Frequently occurs in conjunction with a descending channel or wedge: multi-week lower highs and lower lows. Breakout from these often aligns with the Wedge Pop.

### Trading Strategy
- **Entry** — When price recaptures the tight 10/20 EMA, often by taking out a defined pivot. First clean buy area without picking a bottom. Starts partial, adds on confirmations (breakout from mini-channel or flag), raising stop as he adds.
- **Stop-Loss** — Below near-term price consolidation or low of breakout day. If it's going to work, this low should not be violated.
- **Trailing Stop** — Ride the EMAs. As stock moves up and reconfirms strength, continually raise stop below new pivot lows.
- **Risk/Reward** — Tight stop + emerging trend = favorable R:R.

### Examples
- **NASDAQ/QQQ** — Wedge Pops in indexes signal return to bullish environment.
- **$TSLA** — Wedge Pop (e.g. Point E in his cycle diagram) marked beginning of new uptrend after resistance.
- **$ZM** — Multiple Wedge Pops during pandemic rally.
- **$NVDA** — Wedge Pops with unfilled gaps as sign of institutional buying.
- **AMD** — Wedge Pop occurred the day before the index, highlighting RS.
- **$SHOP** — Wedge Pop when it took out a pivot after tightening under MAs.
- **$FUBO** — "Excellent Wick Play example" within a Bull Flag (a type of Wedge Pop action).

## 9. Cycle of Price Action — Deep Dive

The Cycle is **fractal** — applies across all timeframes from tick to monthly.

### A. Uptrend Cycle (Bottoming to Topping)

**Reversal Extension (Bottoming)**
- *Definition:* Price extended to downside on lower TF while reaching a significant higher TF support (massive weekly base, 200-day SMA). Capitulation; selling pressure largely exhausted.
- *Characteristics:* Heavy volume capitulation on reversal bar. Buyers step in. Oversold.
- *Trading:* Stops below low of reversal bar. First target: 20 EMA. Can also be used tactically on pullbacks in an existing uptrend — look for this on a lower TF (5-min) when a stock pulls back to a daily MA.

**Wedge Pop (First Clean Buy Area)**
- *Definition:* Initial upward move where stock reclaims 10 and 20 EMA after a reversal extension. Tight range + higher low + contracting volatility.
- *Characteristics:* Kell's "money pattern." RS vs index. Tightening 10/20 EMAs. Sometimes breakaway gaps above EMAs.
- *Trading:* Long when price recaptures tightening EMAs. Stop below recent consolidation low. Ride EMAs as trailing stop.

**EMA Crossback (First Retest)**
- *Definition:* First pullback to 10 and 20 EMA after a successful Wedge Pop.
- *Characteristics:* Uptrend established (Wedge Pop done), now returning to EMAs for first support.
- *Trading:* Low-risk entry — buy against 10/20 EMA. Stops just below MAs. Raise trailing stops with EMAs.

**Base n' Break (Consolidation and Breakout)**
- *Definition:* Price consolidates with 10/20 EMA support; forms basing pattern (cup and handle, flat base, VCP, Darvas box). Longer than a 1–3 day pullback.
- *Characteristics:* Kell often pyramids through this phase, adding as stock confirms strength.
- *Trading:* Buy against MAs, add on base breakout. Stops if MAs lost. Ride MAs, take partials on extension from 10 EMA, final stops if EMAs lost.

**Exhaustion Extension (Topping)**
- *Definition:* Blow-off euphoria. Often market top. Price excessively extended from 10 EMA.
- *Characteristics:* Massive volume + frantic chasing = few buyers left. **Second extension** from 10 EMA (since larger base breakout) is a good profit-taking point. **Third extension** is rare and signals very late stages. Extensions from **weekly 10 SMA** (= daily 50 SMA) also key. Gap ups that quickly fade = part of topping.
- *Trading:* Sell into strength. More aggressive on 2nd extension than 1st.

### B. Downtrend Cycle (Topping to Bottoming)

**Wedge Drop (Loss of Momentum)**
- *Definition:* Price decisively drops back down through 10/20 EMA. Loss of momentum; lower highs, lower lows forming.
- *Characteristics:* Wide-range red candle with heavy volume = clear change in character. Gap up that sells down (green-to-red) also signals.
- *Trading:* Get defensive; exit. Buying reason invalidated.

**EMA Crossback (from below)**
- *Definition:* After Wedge Drop, price rallies into 10/20 EMA, gets rejected, reverses down.
- *Characteristics:* Structural lower high. Light volume wedging rally with little underlying power.
- *Trading:* Avoid longs. Shorts can engage.

**Base n' Break (to downside)**
- *Definition:* Consolidation below EMAs, then breaks down, continues lower lows.
- *Trading:* Shorts against MAs, add on breakdown.

**Reversal Extension (Capitulation)** — same as uptrend start; completes cycle.

## 10. Wick Play — Deep Dive

### Definition and Characteristics
- Stock closes off the highs of the day in the previous session, creating a **wick** in the daily bar.
- Next day, stock gaps higher and opens within the range of that previous day's wick.
- Essentially an inside bar, but extremely tight — volatility contraction. Drying volume = very little supply.
- Must stay positive and hold within the wick (no going red during day), meaning buyers support without letting it go red. **If it goes red, the wick play is invalidated.**
- Often seen as a little flag pattern. Best with less volatility and tightening price action.

### Execution
- **Entry:** through the high of the wick bar (inside bar), or through the high of the wick itself.
- **Stop-Loss:** just underneath the low of the entry candlestick / day's low. Very favorable R:R.

### Context and Significance
- Tool to use **in the context of a larger pattern** — not a standalone. It's an additive signal within a broader setup.
- Sign of strength / power — buyers in control.
- Examples: FUBO, Tesla, Nvidia, Zoom, UPST.

## 11. Risk Management — Deep Dive

### Prioritizing Risk
- Put risk first. Always know max loss before entering.
- Trading is 90% about staying in the game long enough to gain experience and trust your strategy.
- **Don't trust stocks, trust your stops.** Stocks can go much lower than anticipated. Better to be out wishing you were in, than in losing money.

### Stop-Loss Placement and Management

**Initial Stop-Loss**
- General: 3–5%. Kell personally: **1–3%**.
- Below a pivot or key MAs.
- If the buying reason is invalidated, sell immediately.

**Rising / Trailing Stops**
- Move stop to **low of breakout day** if strong breakout + heavy volume. If violated = sellers overpowered initial strength.
- **Ignition bars** (wide-range / HV continuation): adjust stops to the low of that bar — those lows shouldn't be violated in a strong trend.
- **Reconfirming Price Strength** — Darvas-like. As stock makes HH/HL, raise stops below new pivot lows on new-high confirmation.
- Uses **10/20 EMA as trailing stops**. Sells when closes below the specific EMA it's been respecting.
- May use rising trendlines (3 touch points) as trailing stop.

**Mental vs. Hard Stops**
- Does not use hard stops for most positions. Mental stops, but knows exactly where they are.
- Prefers manual execution — sometimes stocks briefly dip below stop level then reverse; hard stops would miss.
- In extreme situations or significant news: exits without hesitation.

### Position Sizing and Pyramiding
- **Varying Size** — More risk on highest conviction. Stocks he knows well, strong price action + RS.
- **Initial Position** — Often starts 12–15%, adds 8–10% to reach normal 20–25%. Top ideas: 25–35%.
- **Buying in Pieces** — Key risk management. Adds as stock moves up while raising stop on entire position to BE or profit. Increases size without increasing principal risk. Initial profits finance risk on additions.
- **Risk per Trade** — Generally **≤ 50 bips (0.5%) of portfolio**. Example: risking 1% on a 20% position = 20 bips account risk.

### Avoiding Bad Markets
- **Market Health** — Constantly assess NASDAQ / QQQ. Below 20 EMA → cautious, raise cash, selective; avoid overnight positions.
- **Cash is King** — When market unfavored: cash > hedging. Growth stocks can suffer even if index flat due to weighting of large-caps.
- **Trading Smaller** — Bad markets / losing streaks → trade smaller to regain confidence. **30 Rule:** only 30% of account in troubled times; focus on small, low-risk trades to rebuild.
- **Flexibility** — Adapts; looks at other sectors if money flows there.

### Emotional and Mental Aspects
- **Discipline** — Defined strategy, plan trades; avoids impulsive trades.
- **Learning from Mistakes** — Notes on winners/losers; refines constantly.
- **Emotional Control** — Emotions always present. Admits taking profits too early. Tries to let go of bad days, avoid revenge trades, reset mind.

### Managing Around Earnings
- **Don't Gamble** — Generally advises against.
- **Reduce Position Size** — E.g. trim 40% of position before earnings if no cushion.
- **Small Starter** — For high-conviction names, 2–3% into earnings to make building easier if good.
- **Wait for Confirmation** — Often better: wait for report, then assess price action. If it's going to work, there'll be time to get in.

## 12. Full Setup Catalog (10 Setups)

### 1. Reversal Extension (Capitulation Bottom)
- **Entry:** Extended to downside from 10-day EMA + approaching higher-TF support (weekly base, 200-day SMA, 20-week EMA). Look on lower TF (5m) when daily is at a key MA. Trigger: reversal bar on heavy-volume capitulation.
- **Stop:** Below low of reversal bar.
- **Target:** 20 EMA first. Stops to cost on partials.
- **Works:** TSLA early 2020 (weekly base + 200-day); NVDA 2023 (21 EMA pullback, demand tails on 5m).

### 2. Wedge Pop (Money Pattern)
- **Entry:** Price extended down → tightens in range (higher low) under MAs w/ RS; 10/20 EMAs tightening; recaptures EMAs. Breakaway gaps above EMAs loved.
- **Stop:** Below near-term consolidation / breakout day low.
- **Target:** Ride EMAs as trailing stop.
- **Works:** TSLA early 2020; CRWD; LVGO mid-2020 (RS via higher lows); ZM.
- **Fails:** TSLA Dec 2020 S&P inclusion — gap up → gap down.

### 3. EMA Crossback (Uptrend Pullback)
- **Entry:** After Wedge Pop, first pullback into 10/20 EMA.
- **Stop:** Below MAs.
- **Target:** Ride EMAs.
- **Works:** TSLA mid-2020 (first retest of 20 EMA after Wedge Pop — added); LVGO mid-2020 (10 EMA pullback after inside-bar breakout — sized up).

### 4. Base n' Break (Uptrend Consolidation)
- **Entry:** Price consolidates above 10/20 EMAs for weeks (cup-and-handle, flat base, VCP, Darvas). "Bigger the base, higher in space." Buy against MAs in pieces, add on base breakout.
- **Stop:** Loss of MAs.
- **Target:** Ride MAs, partials on extension from 10 EMA, exit if 10 EMA lost.
- **Works:** TSLA mid-2020 big cup-and-handle; ZM.

### 5. Post-EPS Flag Base (Aggressive)
- **Entry:** Big gap up on earnings (street caught off guard) → bull flag / mini-base. Kell buys into MAs after gap (lower-TF reversal extension near 10 EMA), adds through flag breakout, adds again through pivot high of earnings gap.
- **Stop:** Raised each add; aim for risk-free or reduced principal risk. Low of key breakout bar / pivot.
- **Works:** ASAN ("Almighty base" → earnings → flag); FSLY (positive reaction, 10 EMA support, flag + wick play).

### 6. Descending Channel / Wedge
- **Entry:** Multi-week pattern of LHs and LLs, tightening price action (wedge: LLs become less aggressive). Structural higher lows, shakeout demand tails, gap-down reversal bars at bottom = institutional accumulation. Trigger: Wedge Pop through descending trendline or pivot up on volume. Unfilled gaps = institutional interest.
- **Stop:** Move to cost / pivot low as positions added.
- **Works:** NVDA (gap-down reversal → structural higher low → Wedge Pop through trendline with unfilled gaps).

### 7. Wick Play
- **Entry:** Bar with a wick (closed off highs) → next day gaps higher, opens within the wick, stays positive. Inside bar — ideally drying volume. Must stay green. Use in context of larger pattern.
- **Trigger:** Through high of wick bar (inside bar) or high of the wick.
- **Stop:** Below low of entry candle / low of inside bar. Favorable R:R.
- **Works:** Path; NVDA tight inside bar within wick; UPST; FSLY post-earnings flag with wick play on drying volume.
- **Fails:** LTHM May 24 — gapped up then went red → invalidated.

### 8. Outside Reversal / Gap-Down Reversal Bar
- **Entry:** Uptrending stock pulls back. Entry bar gaps down or trades through prior day's low, then reverses back up through that low within the session. Must be around 10/20 EMA, not extended.
- **Trigger:** As it takes out prior day's low after reversing higher.
- **Stop:** Below low of entry candle.
- **Works:** AMD (bull flag pullback → gap-down reversal at 119–120 near 10/20 EMA); EBAY 1998–99 (gap-down reversal + shakeout demand tails in bases around key MAs).

### 9. Inside Bars
- **Entry:** Bar trades within prior day's range. Multiple consecutive = stronger. Extremely light volume amplifies. Not usually a standalone trigger — a contextual clue for Wedge Pop / Base-n-Break that follows.
- **Stop:** Low of inside bar for breakout that follows.
- **Works:** NVAX (descending wedge tightening around 21 EMA → tight inside bar with drying volume → explosive breakout); CELH (back-to-back inside bars + drying volume after pullback → strong trend along 10/21 EMA).

### 10. Ignition Bar
- **Entry:** Wide-range bar or heavy-volume continuation bar that "stands out like a sore thumb." Strong demand / continuation. Not typically initial entry — confirms strength; add to existing position.
- **Stop:** Low of ignition bar (should not be violated). Adjust as rising stop.
- **Target:** Lows of subsequent ignition bars = progressively higher stops.
- **Works:** RBLX ("ignite bar" after a scummed shooting star); TSLA uptrends with wide-spread candles on HV relative volume.

## Avoiding Traps: Too Tight For Too Long (TTFTL)

Kell cautions against stocks that base in an overly tight formation, becoming "too obvious to everyone." If such a stock underperforms while the index is rising, it can be an "ultimate trap" leading to a violent sell-off. No specific trade example — a psychological pitfall to be aware of.
